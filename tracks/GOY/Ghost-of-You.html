About

This song is about Heathcliff’s famous love with “Grace”, a girl/angel he met over ten years ago, and the two fell into true love. The two fell in love, a love truly like a fairytale, but just before the two could unite in a formal act of love, the girl, “Grace”, went insane. She cut ties with Heathcliff and everyone she ever knew and her family moved away. Just before she left, Heathcliff looked into her eyes for one last time, the beautiful blue eyes he’d known so well before, but to his horror they were brown, hence the lines “What happened, who are you now, your eyes changed from blue to brown” and “Your eyes used to be so blue.” They say the eyes are the window to the soul, and Heathcliff believes her eyes changing was a sign that her soul changed, and since that day he’s considered her to be dead. That presents a unique dilemma, because her body still lives on with the brown-eyed soul. She still wanders this earth. Heathcliff has clung onto the idea for years that maybe the two can reunite and has refused to let himself date anyone else for that very reason. This song explores Heathcliff’s internal conflict about whether he is truly in love with the girl left on the earth, or just her “ghost” in Heaven. Should he move on? Is she dead and buried? Or is there a chance that they can still reunite in this world? Lines like “Can’t live, can’t die without you” explore this very question. Heathcliff cannot live without Grace. At the same time, his life isn’t complete without her and he can’t yet die. He can’t do anything. He’s stuck, hence the apparent stark contradiction between “Can’t live, can’t die without you”. 

Heathcliff was not Heathcliff’s birth name. He changed it to “Heathcliff” after this affair with Grace. He figured that his old self died when Grace died and, accordingly, he needed a new name. The name “Heathcliff” comes from the novel “Wuthering Heights” where the two main characters, “Heathcliff” and “Catherine” share uncanny similarities between Heathcliff and Grace. In the novel, Heathcliff’s true love dies and he spends the rest of his life obsessed with her ghost. In a famous scene, he runs to a window and screams out her name, to which her ghost appears to him. Meditating on this song, Heathcliff pictures himself in a window screaming out to Grace’s ghost, hence the lines

“I stare outside, waiting for a sound

The air calls your name with every breath I breathe

I lie in bed for hours but I never can sleep

I scream at night, call you back to me”

Just before Grace came into Heathcliff’s life, he had a major personal development where he found God and turned his life around from a dark and depressed path. Heathcliff considers himself to have been “born again” at this point. It was at this point that Grace and Heathcliff became friends. The period from finding God until Grace going crazy was by far the best point in Heathcliff’s life. The name “Grace” is of course derived from the Christian concept of “God’s Grace.” Is it just coincidence that in the period where Heathcliff received miraculous Grace from God that Grace was by his side the whole time? And that as soon as she left these miraculous graces also left? Heathcliff believes that her soul was that of an angel temporarily manifesting on this earth for the sake of bestowing God's graces on Heathcliff. That is why after a year of love and infatuation, when they finally were about to date, she went crazy and her soul changed. Heathcliff, being mortal, could not date an angel, so God had to call her back to Heaven. 

Interestingly, half a decade later, the body of Grace enrolled at the same university where Heathcliff was enrolled. Just before graduating, in April 2021, he posted a love letter around his university campus asking the very question of this song: “Do I love you or ghost of you?”

The letter was received very well by many other students, but only grace would know who it was from or who it was for. Here are the contents of that letter.

“This is my love letter.

I know this is going to come off as really weird, but I don’t how else to do this. I’m about to graduate, but I can’t move forward with regrets and my biggest regret is you. I saw you once here at UF and you saw me, but I was scared to say anything. After that, I was hoping I’d bump into you again naturally, but it never happened. I don’t how to reach out to you without coming off as overbearing, so yes, I decided to write an anonymous love letter, post it all around campus, and have faith that only you would know who I’m speaking to.

To be blunt, I’m desperate. Not desperate as the term is normally used, but I’m desperate for you and only you. For this reason, I haven’t allowed myself to go on a single first date through my entire high school and university career, no matter how many times I’ve been asked, and I’ve been asked many MANY times by many nice girls. I may eventually have to settle for one of them, but for now I’m reaching for my dream, which is you. I pray that you’ll see this letter and maybe I can see you once more.

I think the best way to reach you is to use your own words. You sent me this text back in 8th grade, and I’ve cherished these words ever since.

“One day, we are going to be adults and shift and have careers and spouses and kids(maybe idk you do what you want) and one day we are going to look back on our lives and how silly everything was, and how we made big deals over small things and shift, and how we are going to grow up. And we are going to be completely different people, and tell stories about our middle school years, and see things in a whole different light, and just get older. And we won't be here forever. And one day, we are going to wish we were back in eighth grade but we will never be able to go back”

It’s been seven years and without a doubt, we’ve both changed and matured, and I definitely look back and laugh at how silly I was! It’s also true that I wish I could go back to eighth grade and be with you, but I can’t. But we’re here now and there’s no reason why we can’t try things again as adults. Again, were both different, so it may not work out and that’s fine, but you were the single most important thing to ever happen to me so I can’t move forward without at least trying to reunite.

They say the eyes are windows to the soul. I remember how just before you left the color of your eyes changed from blue to brown. I figured that meant that your soul changed, and you became a new person. Now I simply want to look into your eyes once more and see if I’m in love with you or with the ghost of you.

You know who I am and if you’re willing, I’m sure you can figure out how to contact me.”

You can see photos of the original letters on Heathcliff’s Instagram “Heathcliffbeefcliff” in the post dating back to April 2021.

 

Fun fact: the inspiration for the music was actually the Juice WRLD song, “Come and Go”, but the sound ended up being totally different, and Heathcliff thinks it is perfect. 

 

Heathcliff had a really hard time recording this song since it was such a different style than what he is used to, but it clearly paid off. Each articulation has so much passion and emotion behind it. For example, hear the line “even more”. There is a shake in Heathcliff’s voice highlighting how he cracks under the intense emotion. Also see how he articulates “sound” in “waiting for a sound”. He does a strong whisper contrasting all the lines before it, so that it stands out like the wind howling in the night. 

Overall, Heathcliff rips into each word he sings, adding true passion to the song. At first, Heathcliff’s vocal chords got worn out very quickly while recording, so he had to try rerecording it on multiple occasions, but now Heathcliff can rip it out at any time or place. 

 

This is Heathcliff’s first serious song and the first song to display the deeper side of Heathcliff.

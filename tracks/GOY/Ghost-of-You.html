Annotations

 [1]This is a genuine appeal to the living body of Grace to see if any of the old angel that Heathcliff used to love still remains, or is she truly dead and should he try to move on?

 [2]The girl in question had blue eyes until she went crazy, at which point her eyes turned brown.

 [3]"Grace" is the girl's name. Also, “my grace" is a phrase similar to "[oh] my goodness". This line has the dual meaning.

 [4]In a famous text message to Heathcliff, Grace said that they were just kids and that they would look back and laugh at how insignificant their affair was, but in reality, ten years later, Heathcliff loves her even more.

 [5]"window to your soul" refers to the eyes, so, "wipe the tears from your eyes." This is particularly significant, because in the following lines discussing how her eyes changed from blue to brown, it implies that her soul changed as well.

 [6]The girl in question had blue eyes until she went crazy, at which point her eyes turned brown, which Heathcliff believes is evidence that her soul changed and that she is now a new person.

 

About

This song is about Heathcliff’s famous love affair with a girl/angel he met over ten years ago but who then went crazy. Her eyes changed from blue to brown and Heathcliff has ever since considered her soul to have “died” leaving behind just a shell of the person she used to be. To this day Heathcliff still loves her, but he wonders, “do I love you or just the ghost of you?”

Annotations

 [1]This does not mean "go all night" because Heathcliff believes it is a one way journey. Rather, the "morning" is the first light of the other side after escaping the world of darkness.

 

About

The song has several meanings to Heathcliff, and he leaves it up to you to discern your own meaning from it. The song sounds like it is discussing escaping into heaven through death, but that is one meaning Heathcliff certainly didn't intend, although it is still valid. Heathcliff himself prefers interpretations that have implications for us carrying out our living lives. There is a slideshow music video that conveys the story Heathcliff had in mind, check it out to get the full experience!

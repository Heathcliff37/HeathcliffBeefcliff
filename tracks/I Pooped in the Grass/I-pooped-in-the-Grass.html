About
 Despite popular belief, “I pooped in the Grass” is not about a dog.
Heathcliff was sitting the porch of his grandfather’s house practicing harmonica while his young cousin was playing nearby. While practicing a new riff, he decided to sing the words “I pooped I the toilet” to entertain his cousin. She then jokingly remarked about how the outhouse at the cabin he would soon be staying at was infested with spiders and she suggested that he would have to “poop in the grass”. Heathcliff’s singing then followed suite, and the hit song was born!
Heathcliff was initially hesitant to release the song. He was somewhat embarrassed, but when one of his close friend’s heard it almost a year after its creation, he encouraged Heathcliff to release it, which he did. Heathcliff eventually published it on YouTube. Soon thereafter it was cross posted an obscure subreddit and then, unbeknownst to Heathcliff, picked up by Tosh.0. They gave Heathcliff no notice that his song and video would be featured. One day he received a flood of views and comments on his YouTube video with the common theme “Tosh.0 brought me here”. At first he thought it was some kind of prank, but after staying up all night to watch a rerun, he confirmed that he had indeed just been featured on the highest rated show on prime time national television. Unfortunately, Heathcliff received zero compensation from that feature performance, but it inspired him to pursue music professionally after he graduated from university!
The deep raspy vocals, particularly prominent in the original video and recoding, are the result of an excess of air being built up in the lungs. While playing chugging rhythms on harmonica, air is almost exclusively drawn into the lungs, so the musician ends up needing to rapidly expel air. That high volume of air supports a lush powerful vocal tone. The “oow!… Woohoo!” interjections are part of another technique called “whopping” used to expel excess air from the lungs. “Whooping” also adds to a fun and exciting performance!

The song was originally released under the name “Heathcliff Bourdeau” before Heathcliff changed his last name to “Beefcliff.”

Annotations
 [1]This means “I love you”. Heathcliff learned “I love you” from flirting with Russian classmates
 [2]This means “I don’t know”. Heathcliff studied Russian in university and learned this phrase since it was his standard answer to every question the professor would ask.
 [3]This means “fish” which Heathcliff learned because he loves fish. He even holds a degree in marine science.
 [4] This is a comedic misdirection. The listener expects Heathcliff to be talking about guns per the typical rap and gangster culture, but in reality “shot” refers to procreation between Heathcliff and his ideal Russian mail-order bride.
 [5]Biden issued an executive order blocking all K-1 Visa’s. At the time Heathcliff was trying to help his Ukrainian mail order bride immigrate to America. The line is a tongue in cheek jab at the Biden administration.
 [6]The Russo-Ukrainian war of 2022. Heathcliff had plans to visit Ukraine and bring back a mail order bride, but the war interrupted those plans.
 [7]Barnacles have the longest penis to body ratio out of any animal, hence Heathcliff is implying that his penis is long enough to reach all the way to Ukraine or Russia.
 [8]These are the ideal characteristics that Heathcliff looks for in a mail-order bride.
 
 
About
Heathcliff wanted to make a rap song using the entirety of his knowledge of Russian after studying it for two semesters. He only learned how to say "I love you", "I don't know", "fish", and the common swear words "cyka blyat".
He learned "I love you" from flirting with Russian classmates, "I don't know" since it was his standard answer to every question the professor would ask, and "fish" due to his passion for fish.
The original song was recorded entirely using mouth sounds that Heathcliff did himself. Afterwards, he decided to expand the song and add verses in English. The goal of the verses and the whole song was to highlight how easy it is to make a rap song that is acceptable by today's standards. Needless to say, the lyrics turned out quite witty and impressive in and of themselves. Heathcliff's producer further added EDM elements to later remixes.

There is an official dance accompanying this song. See the artist's official page. https://youtube.com/shorts/nD0nX55Y5MY?feature=share


Heathcliff intentionally wrote the Russian verses using Google translate. See the translated version below to see what they mean.
